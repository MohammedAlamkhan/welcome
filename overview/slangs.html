Overview

Mumbai, known for its fast-paced life, is equally famous for its unique street slangs. The Bambaiya language, a mix of Hindi, Marathi, and tapori (street) dialects, reflects the city’s vibrant, diverse culture. Here's a look at some of the most common slangs heard in the city.

Popular Mumbai Slangs

  • Bindaas: Cool, carefree, or relaxed. Used when someone is bold or fearless.
  • Apun: Meaning “I” or “me,” often used in casual or informal conversation.
  • Jhakaas: Excellent or awesome. It's a popular term for something really impressive.
  • Cutting Chai: Half a glass of tea, typical in Mumbai tea stalls.
  • Tension Nahi Leneka: Don’t worry, often used to calm someone down in tense situations.
  • Bhidu: Friend or bro, often used among close buddies.
  • Jugaad: A quick, creative solution or hack to solve a problem in an unconventional way.

Street Slangs

  • Kaiko: Why, typically used in a questioning tone, like "Why are you doing this?"
  • Haila: A popular expression of shock or surprise, often used as “Oh no!”
  • Pakao: To bore someone, or when something is tedious or irritating.
  • Fattu: Refers to someone who is a coward or easily scared.
  • Item: A term used for a person (usually in a flirtatious or teasing manner).
