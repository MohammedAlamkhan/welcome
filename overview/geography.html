Overview

Mumbai, located on the western coast of India, is a city built on what was originally an archipelago of seven islands. Over centuries, these islands were joined through land reclamation projects to form the current landmass.

Location

Mumbai is situated on the Konkan coast along the Arabian Sea. It lies at approximately 18.96° N latitude and 72.82° E longitude. The city covers an area of about 603 square kilometers.

Topography

The topography of Mumbai is characterized by its coastal and low-lying areas. The northern part of the city has hilly regions, including the Borivali National Park, while the southern part is flatter and heavily urbanized. The highest point in Mumbai is at 450 meters in the northern hills.

Climate

Mumbai has a tropical climate, specifically a tropical wet and dry climate. The city experiences four main seasons: summer (March to June), monsoon (June to September), post-monsoon (October to November), and winter (December to February). The average annual rainfall is around 2,200 mm, with most of it occurring during the monsoon season.

Natural Resources

Mumbai's natural resources include its coastal and marine biodiversity, the Sanjay Gandhi National Park, and various urban forests and mangroves. The city's coastal location provides significant economic advantages through its ports and fishing industry.

Urban Geography

Mumbai's urban geography is marked by its high population density and extensive metropolitan area. The city's urban sprawl extends into the Mumbai Metropolitan Region (MMR), which includes surrounding areas such as Thane, Navi Mumbai, and Vasai-Virar. The central business district is located in South Mumbai, while suburban areas spread to the north.
