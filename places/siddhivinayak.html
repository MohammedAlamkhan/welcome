Overview

Siddhivinayak Temple, dedicated to Lord Ganesha, is one of the most revered temples in Mumbai. Located in Prabhadevi, the temple attracts thousands of devotees daily who come to seek blessings from the elephant-headed deity. The temple is renowned for its beautiful architecture and the deep spiritual significance it holds for Hindus.

Location

Siddhivinayak Temple, SK Bole Marg, Prabhadevi, Mumbai, Maharashtra 400028, India

History

The Siddhivinayak Temple was originally built in 1801 by Laxman Vithu and Deubai Patil. The temple has grown in significance over the years and is now one of the richest temples in Mumbai. It is particularly famous for the idol of Lord Ganesha, which is believed to be self-manifested, and for the temple's miraculous powers in fulfilling devotees' wishes.

Architecture

The temple features a unique architectural design with a gold-plated dome and the wooden doors carved with images of the Ashtavinayak, the eight manifestations of Ganesha. The sanctum sanctorum houses the idol of Siddhivinayak, made from a single black stone, with the trunk turned to the right, which is considered to be unusual and highly sacred.

Visitor Information

Siddhivinayak Temple is open to devotees from 5:30 AM to 9:50 PM daily. Tuesdays are particularly busy due to the special aarti performed on this day. Devotees can offer prayers, participate in the daily aartis, and seek the blessings of Lord Ganesha. It is recommended to visit early in the morning to avoid long queues.
