Overview

The BMC (Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation) Building, also known as the Municipal Corporation Building, is an iconic structure in Mumbai. Located opposite the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus, the building serves as the headquarters of the Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai. It is one of the most recognized landmarks of the city, symbolizing the administrative strength of Mumbai.

Location

BMC Building, Mahapalika Marg, Fort, Mumbai, Maharashtra 400001, India

History

The BMC Building was completed in 1893, designed by architect Frederick William Stevens, who also designed the nearby Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus. The building was constructed in the Gothic Revival style, reflecting the architectural trends of the British colonial era. It has since become a symbol of Mumbai's municipal governance and heritage.

Architecture

The BMC Building is an architectural marvel with its grand facade, intricately carved details, and imposing tower. The structure combines Venetian Gothic and Indo-Saracenic architectural styles, with influences seen in its arches, domes, and spires. The building is crowned by a central dome, which is a distinctive feature of its design, and the interiors are just as elaborate with high ceilings and beautiful stonework.

Visitor Information

While the BMC Building is primarily an administrative office and not open for public tours, it is a must-see for its architectural grandeur. Visitors can admire the exterior of the building and its surroundings. The area is well-connected by public transport, with the nearest landmark being the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus. It is recommended to visit during the day to fully appreciate the building's architectural details.
