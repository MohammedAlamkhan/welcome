Overview

The Nita Mukesh Ambani Cultural Centre (NMACC) is a state-of-the-art cultural venue located in the heart of Mumbai, India. Established by the Reliance Foundation, the centre aims to promote Indian arts and culture while also providing a platform for global cultural exchange. With its world-class infrastructure, NMACC hosts a variety of events, including theatre, music, dance, and visual arts.

Location

Nita Mukesh Ambani Cultural Centre, Jio World Centre, Bandra-Kurla Complex, Mumbai, Maharashtra 400051, India

History

Inaugurated in 2023, the NMACC was founded by Nita Ambani as part of her vision to create a world-class cultural centre in India. The centre reflects her passion for the arts and her commitment to making cultural experiences accessible to a wide audience. Since its opening, NMACC has quickly become a landmark in Mumbai's cultural landscape, attracting artists and audiences from around the globe.

Architecture

The NMACC is a modern architectural marvel with sleek lines, spacious interiors, and cutting-edge facilities. Designed to host a variety of cultural events, the centre includes multiple performance spaces, including a grand theatre, studio theatre, and an art gallery. The building's design seamlessly blends functionality with aesthetics, creating an inviting and inspiring environment for both performers and visitors.

Visitor Information

The NMACC is open to the public and hosts a variety of events throughout the year, including performances, exhibitions, and workshops. Visitors are encouraged to check the official website for the latest event schedule. The centre is easily accessible from various parts of Mumbai and offers ample parking facilities. It is recommended to book tickets in advance for popular events.
