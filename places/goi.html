Overview

The Gateway of India is an iconic arch monument built during the 20th century in Mumbai, India. It overlooks the Arabian Sea and is one of the city's most popular tourist destinations.

Location

Apollo Bandar, Colaba, Mumbai, Maharashtra 400001, India

History

The Gateway of India was built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911. The foundation stone was laid in 1913, and the structure was completed in 1924. The monument is a blend of Hindu and Muslim architectural styles, with elements of Roman triumphal arches.

Nearby Attractions

There are several attractions near the Gateway of India, including the Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, the Colaba Causeway Market, and the Elephanta Caves, which are a short ferry ride away.

Visitor Information

The Gateway of India is open to visitors 24 hours a day, and there is no entry fee. It is best visited early in the morning or late in the evening to avoid the crowds and enjoy the serene views of the Arabian Sea.
