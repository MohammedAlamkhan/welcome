Overview

Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (CST), formerly known as Victoria Terminus, is a historic railway station in Mumbai. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its stunning Victorian Gothic Revival architecture and its significant role in Mumbai's history.

Location

DN Road, Fort, Mumbai, Maharashtra 400001, India

History

Built in 1887 to commemorate the Golden Jubilee of Queen Victoria, CST was designed by Frederick William Stevens. The station has been a crucial part of Mumbai's railway network and is an iconic symbol of the city’s colonial heritage.

Architecture

The station’s architecture is a blend of Victorian Gothic Revival and traditional Indian styles. Its ornate façade, grand dome, and intricate detailing make it one of the most visually impressive railway stations in the world.

Visitor Information

Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus is a functional railway station, so visitors can explore its architectural beauty while using the services provided. Guided tours are available to provide deeper insights into its history and design. Ensure to respect the station’s operational environment while visiting.
