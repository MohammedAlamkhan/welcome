Overview

Marine Drive, also known as the Queen's Necklace, is a 3.6-kilometer-long boulevard located in South Mumbai. It runs along the coastline and offers breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea. It is a popular spot for both locals and tourists to take leisurely walks, enjoy the sea breeze, and watch the sunset.

Location

Marine Drive, South Mumbai, Mumbai, Maharashtra 400020, India

History

Marine Drive was constructed in the early 20th century, transforming the coastline into a grand promenade. The project, initiated by the then Governor of Bombay, Sir Pherozeshah Mehta, was aimed at improving the city's waterfront. Over time, Marine Drive has become a landmark of Mumbai, known for its stunning night views when the city lights resemble a string of pearls.

Architecture

The boulevard features a wide, curving road flanked by a series of art deco buildings, which were constructed in the 1930s and 1940s. The architectural style is reflective of the period's influence, with clean lines and streamlined forms. The smooth, clean design of the promenade itself complements the elegant structures, making Marine Drive a visual delight.

Visitor Information

Marine Drive is open to the public 24/7, making it a perfect spot for early morning walks or late-night strolls. There are numerous benches along the promenade where visitors can relax and enjoy the view. Nearby, there are various eateries and cafes for refreshments. It's recommended to visit during the evening to experience the beautiful sunset and the illuminated cityscape. The area is generally safe, but visitors should be mindful of their belongings and surroundings.
