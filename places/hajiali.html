Overview

Haji Ali Dargah is a renowned mosque and tomb located on an islet off the coast of Worli in Mumbai. The structure is a revered pilgrimage site and is notable for its stunning Islamic architecture and serene setting, accessible via a narrow causeway that gets submerged during high tide.

Location

Haji Ali Dargah, Haji Ali Road, Worli, Mumbai, Maharashtra 400030, India

History

Haji Ali Dargah was built in the early 15th century by a wealthy merchant named Haji Ali, who was said to have been on a pilgrimage to Mecca when he died. His remains were brought back to Mumbai, and the dargah was constructed in his honor. It has since become a significant site for both Muslim and non-Muslim devotees.

Architecture

The dargah is an exemplary example of Indo-Islamic architecture. It features a white marble structure with exquisite minarets and a grand dome. The intricate designs and carvings add to the mosque's aesthetic appeal, while its location on a small island enhances its tranquility and beauty.

Visitor Information

Visitors can reach Haji Ali Dargah via a causeway that connects the islet to the mainland. The dargah is open to the public from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM. During high tide, access to the islet may be restricted, so it is advisable to check local tide timings before planning a visit. Proper attire is required for entry, and visitors are encouraged to be respectful of the religious significance of the site.
